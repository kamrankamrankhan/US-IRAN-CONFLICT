Overview of the US-Iran conflict

The United States and Iran have been locked in a tense standoff for decades, with relations hitting new lows in 2026. This site offers a comprehensive analysis of the crisis, including military developments, sanctions, nuclear diplomacy, and regional fallout. Understanding the roots of the conflict and current events is essential for anyone following Middle East geopolitics.

Background and context

US-Iran relations have been strained since the 1979 Islamic Revolution and the hostage crisis. Decades of sanctions, proxy conflicts, and failed nuclear negotiations have deepened mistrust. In 2026, tensions have escalated further amid renewed military action, stricter sanctions, and stalled talks over Iran’s nuclear program. Regional actors, including Israel and Gulf states, are deeply involved in the dynamics of the crisis.

Key topics we cover

Operation Epic Fury refers to the coordinated military operations that have defined the current phase of the conflict. We analyze its objectives, timeline, and consequences. Iran’s nuclear program remains at the center of international concern; we track developments in enrichment, inspections, and diplomacy. Military buildup on both sides—naval deployments, missile systems, and regional bases—is documented and explained. Economic sanctions have crippled Iran’s economy and reshaped global energy markets; we examine their scope and impact. Proxy networks extend the conflict across the Middle East, from Yemen to Syria to Iraq. Finally, we assess the regional impact on stability, refugees, and alliances.
